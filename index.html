Color blindness types

Red-blind

~1% of males

Reds appear dark or black. Red-green combinations become impossible to distinguish.

Green-blind

~6% of males — most common

The most common form. Greens appear yellowish, red-green confusion is severe.

Blue-blind

<0.01% — rarest

Blues appear greenish, yellows appear violet. Very rare but severely impacts blue/yellow UI.

Frequently asked questions

VisionX is a color blindness accessibility toolkit from ATA IT Solutions. It helps you simulate how interfaces and imagery appear to people with different types of color vision deficiency, and check contrast against WCAG guidelines.

VisionX uses established RGB→LMS transforms and dichromacy simulation matrices (Brettel/Viénot style) for Protanopia, Deuteranopia, and Tritanopia. Results are a strong approximation for design review; clinical diagnosis always requires a professional.
